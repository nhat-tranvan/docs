## Prerequisites

- A cloud connection with the permissions your agents need to assess resources.
- Resources already discovered in CloudThinker; assessments only evaluate discovered resources.
- Optional: tag or resource filters ready if you want to scope the run.

<Note>
  Assessments are limited to discovered resources. CloudKeepers automations
  still operate across any resources you permit in your cloud connection.
</Note>

---

## Step-by-Step Guide

### Step 1: Create an Assessment

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Assessment and start a run">
    Go to **Assessment** and click **Create Assessment**.

    <Frame>
      <img
        src="/images/infrastructure/assessments/1-assessment-landing-cta.jpg"
        alt="Assessment landing with create CTA and overview timeline"
      />
    </Frame>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Name your assessment">
    Enter a clear name and optional description, then choose **Create**.

    <Frame>
      <img
        src="/images/infrastructure/assessments/2-assessment-create-modal.jpg"
        alt="Modal to name and describe a new assessment"
      />
    </Frame>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Find your draft">
    Your new assessment appears in the list with status **Pending**. Click **Run assessment** to configure it.

    <Frame>
      <img
        src="/images/infrastructure/assessments/3-assessment-list-run.jpg"
        alt="Assessment list showing a pending assessment with run action"
      />
    </Frame>
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Step 2: Configure the Assessment

<Steps>
  <Step title="Select Well-Architected pillars">
    Pick the pillars to evaluate (e.g., Cost Optimization, Security, Reliability, Performance Efficiency, Operational Excellence, Sustainability).

    <Frame>
      <img
        src="/images/infrastructure/assessments/4-assessment-select-pillars.jpg"
        alt="Pillar selection step for the assessment"
      />
    </Frame>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Select resources to assess">
    Choose the discovered resources you want to include. You can scope to categories or specific resources. Only discovered resources are assessable.

    <Frame>
      <img
        src="/images/infrastructure/assessments/5-assessment-select-resources.jpg"
        alt="Resource selection step with filters and toggles"
      />
    </Frame>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Review and start">
    Confirm pillars and selected resources, then click **Start Assessment**.

    <Frame>
      <img
        src="/images/infrastructure/assessments/6-assessment-summary.jpg"
        alt="Assessment summary before starting"
      />
    </Frame>
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Step 3: Review Results and Take Action

<Steps>
  <Step title="View analytics and findings">
    After the run, review analytics for findings by pillar, potential savings, and scope.

    <Frame>
      <img
        src="/images/infrastructure/assessments/7-assessment-analytics-dashboard.jpg"
        alt="Analytics dashboard with findings by pillar and savings by service"
      />
    </Frame>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Drill into recommendations">
    Expand recommendations by resource to see effort, risk, and remediation details you can action.

    <Frame>
      <img
        src="/images/infrastructure/assessments/8-assessment-recommendations.jpg"
        alt="Recommendations list grouped by resource with effort and risk"
      />
    </Frame>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Open recommendation details">
    Click a recommendation to view impact analysis, savings, and implementation guidelines.

    <Frame>
      <img
        src="/images/infrastructure/shared/9-assessment-recommendation-detail.jpg"
        alt="Recommendation detail with impact analysis and implementation guidelines"
      />
    </Frame>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Take action">
    Choose how to proceed: run Impact Analytics, generate guidelines, ask a custom prompt, or implement directly. Click **Start** to launch the action.

    <Frame>
      <img
        src="/images/infrastructure/shared/10-assessment-action-modal.jpg"
        alt="Action modal with options for analytics, guidelines, prompts, and implementation"
      />
    </Frame>
  </Step>
</Steps>
