Convention Rules keep the AI reviewer aligned with your team's standards in two ways: it reads the convention files already in your repository, and it learns reusable rules automatically from your past code reviews and applies them going forward.

---

## How it works

- **Your existing convention files.** Point the reviewer at the coding-assistant convention files your team already maintains (for example `CLAUDE.md` or `.cursorrules`). The reviewer reads them during each review, so no rule authoring is required.
- **Rules distilled from past reviews.** On a schedule you choose, CloudThinker studies the review findings your team has already resolved on a repository, distills the recurring ones into named rules, and applies those rules in future reviews. You manage the results from the **Rules** tab.

The two mechanisms complement each other. You can use either on its own or both together.

### Custom convention paths

You can add **Custom Convention Paths** to point the reviewer at additional convention files or directories in your repository. This is useful when your team keeps convention files in non-standard locations or uses more than one tool.

Click **+ Add path** in the Convention Rules dialog to specify relative paths to additional convention files or directories.

## Rules distilled from past reviews

CloudThinker learns your team's coding conventions from each repository's default branch and applies them in future reviews. As your team resolves review findings, CloudThinker distills the recurring ones into named rules. New rules appear automatically after the next scheduled run, so the reviewer keeps catching the issues your team cares about without anyone writing rules by hand.

### Schedule distillation

Distillation runs per repository on a cadence you set. In **Code Review → Repositories**, use the **Convention Rules** column to choose how often CloudThinker refreshes a repository's rules:

| Option | Behavior |
|--------|----------|
| **Off** | Do not distill rules for this repository |
| **Weekly** | Refresh rules once a week |
| **Monthly** | Refresh rules once a month |

The column tooltip sums it up: *How often resolved review findings are distilled into this repo's coding rules.*

<Info>
  The schedule is available once Code Review is enabled for the repository and the repository has finished cloning. A few providers do not support distilled rules yet, in which case the option is disabled with a note.
</Info>

### The Rules tab

Distilled rules are managed from the **Rules** tab under **Code Review** ("Team conventions distilled from past reviews"). You don't hand-write rules here; the tab is where you review, filter, and enable or disable the rules CloudThinker has learned.

| Column | What it shows |
|--------|---------------|
| **Rule** | The rule's name and a short slug |
| **Repository** | The repository the rule was distilled from |
| **Cited** | How many review findings have referenced this rule |
| **Enable** | A toggle to enable or disable an individual rule |

Use the filters at the top of the table to narrow the list:

- **Search** by slug or title.
- **Repository** to focus on one or more repositories.
- **Status** to show only **Active** or **Disabled** rules.

Until the first run completes, the tab shows an empty state explaining that rules appear automatically after the next scheduled run. Disable any rule you don't want the reviewer to apply, and re-enable it later at any time.

---

## Permissions

Managing convention rules requires the appropriate workspace permission. Without it, the Rules tab reports that you don't have permission to view convention rules and to contact your workspace admin. Ask a workspace admin to grant access if you need to view rules or change a repository's schedule.
